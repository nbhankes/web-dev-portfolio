What is dimensional anlysis? Let's look at this fun example, so you can appreciate some of what the software does behind the scenes.

×

Ever wonder how many skittles fit in an Olympic size swimming pool?

Yeah, I know. Me too!

With the help of dimensional analysis, we can find an answer.

But first we need to know a couple things.

1. The volume of a Skittle

2. The volume of an Olymic sized swimmming pool.

Let's say a single Skittle displaces 4 milliliters of water in a graduated cylinder.

And Google is so kind as to tell me that an Olympic sized swimming pool holds 2,500,000 liters.

Each of those last two statment is a conversion factor. And dimensional analysis makes calculations using these types of conversion factors:

1 Skittle

4 mL

2,500,000 liters

1 Olympic Pool

And since units found in both the numerator (top) and denominator (bottom) cancel one another out, we'll add another conversion factor to cancel out the milliliters and liters units. And then we will multiply across and divide to find our answer.

4 milliliters

625,000,000 Skittles

1 Olympic Pool

Unlike current online conversion tools, this program allows users to generate and save their own conversion factors, which would make this application useful for laboratories, researchers, and many others in engineering and the sciences.

The calculator is functional, and I plan to add addtional functionality in the future, such as authorization and authentication, billing, and more tooling for researchers to save calculations and collaborate on projects.

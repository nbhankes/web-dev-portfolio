What is dimensional analysis? Let's look at this fun example, so you can understand what's happening under the hood.

×

Ever wonder how many skittles fit in an Olympic size swimming pool?

Yeah, I know. Me too!

With the help of dimensional analysis, we can find an answer.

But first we need to know a couple things.

1. The volume of a Skittle

2. The volume of an Olympic sized swimmming pool

Let's say a single Skittle displaces 4 milliliters of water in a graduated cylinder.

And Google says an Olympic sized swimming pool holds 2,500,000 liters.

Each of those last two statments is a conversion factor. And dimensional analysis makes calculations using these types of conversion factors:

1 Skittle

per

4 mL

2,500,000 liters

per

1 Olympic Pool

And since units found in both the numerator (top) and denominator (bottom) cancel out one another, we'll add another conversion factor to cancel out the milliliters and liters units, leaving us with Skittles per Olympic Pool.

Follow the rules of fractions by multiplying across and dividing to find our product value.

Unlike current online conversion tools, this program allows users to generate and save their own conversion factors, which makes this application useful for research and engineering firms.

The calculator is functional, and I plan to add authorization and authentication, billing, and more tooling for users to save calculations and collaborate on projects.
